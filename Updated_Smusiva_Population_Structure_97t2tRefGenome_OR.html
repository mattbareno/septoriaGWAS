Updated Population Genomics of S. musiva

2023-04-04

Background

Past research (Tabima et al.2019) Has explored the population structure of North American S. musiva populations. In an attempt to understand the broader population structure, two types of population structure analyses were conducted:
1. An exploratory method where the best number of clusters to model the entire populations pattern of genetic variation.
2. A method where the number of clusters is set to the number of source regions to test the accuracy of these regions as clusters and to see a finer scale of hypothetical admixture among the populations and their isolates.

Replication of past Research

Following the methods and data of Tabima et al., a replication of their results were attempted. Below is the replication for the exploratory method, where three broad clusters best fit the observed population structure. Furthermore, assignments for each individual were consistent. New Reference Genome

With the methods solidified, the same population with the new reference genome could be analyzed. The reference genome could affect the results through one or both of two ways:
1. The effect of a new reference genome (MN-14) could affect assignments for each individual.
2. The effect of a more comprehensive genome. The new MN-14 reference genome was genotyped telomere-to-telomore. Increasing the number of variants to more sites on the genome could change the pattern of results.
The exploratory method yielded broadly the same pattern of results, with three clusters also fitting the population structure. However, three isolates were assigned partial membership of another cluster, potentially indicating some gene flow among the two populations.

New Reference Genome & New Oregon Isolates

New Isolates were added in order to better represent NA populations. Undersampling existing populations in a broader region can indicate misleading population structure and lead to improper inferences. Filling in the gap between British Columbia and the rest of North America, Oregon isolates were added.
The exploratory method also found three clusters, and the Oregon isolates were assigned the same cluster as the distinct BC2 cluster found from the previous literature.
